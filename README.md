# Romboutswervepolder: Rewetting with Weirs – Water Quality

## Overview
This project was completed as part of the Erasmus Mundus 
Master's in Applied Ecohydrology (MAEH) at the University 
of Antwerp, Belgium (December 2024).

## Study Area
Romboutswervepolder is a ~700 ha historical polder in West 
Flanders, Belgium, designated as a European Special 
Protection Zone (SPA-V).

## Objectives
- Identify key water management and quality challenges
- Analyse seasonal water quality parameters
- Recommend nature-based and adaptive management solutions

## My Contributions
- Current challenges in Romboutswervepolder
- Future recommendations

## Data Source
Water quality data (salinity, EC, TDS, dissolved oxygen, 
precipitation) was sourced from the Damme/FluxSense 
monitoring dashboard provided by project stakeholders.
Data link: https://dashboard.fluxsense.app/

## Key Parameters Analysed
- Salinity (PSU)
- Electric Conductivity (μS/cm)
- Total Dissolved Solids (mg/L)
- pH
- Dissolved Oxygen (mg/L)
- Precipitation (mm)

## Key Findings
- Salinity and EC showed strong seasonal variation driven 
  by precipitation-driven dilution
- DO levels occasionally dropped below 5 mg/L, indicating 
  conditions harmful to aquatic life
- Weir height management and nature-based solutions 
  recommended for long-term resilience
